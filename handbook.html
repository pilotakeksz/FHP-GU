Department Handbook

This page contains all core policies and procedures of the FHP Ghost Unit. Review all sections before your first shift. This documents is subject to change with notice.

1.1 Conduct

As a member of the Ghost Unit, you are expected to uphold the highest standards of professionalism and on duty, and off duty in uniform. This is includes but is not limited to: general respect towards all others, professionalism in all interactions, adherence to all department policies and server rules, etc.

Ghost Unit uniforms may be worn off-duty only when not in roleplay. We trust our troopers to respect this policy. Failure to do so may result in infractions.

You are representing not only yourself but the entire Ghost Unit's reputation. Any actions bringing disrepute to the deparmtent may result in disciplinary action, including removal if severe.

1.2 Vehicle Regulations

All available vehicle customization options are listed in the Vehicle Guidelines tab. All accessories listed must be installed as described in the guidelines. Any accessory not listed, is by default not permitted.

1.3 Weapon Regulations

Unless stated otherwise for specialised units, all personnel are required to carry the Glock 17 as their primary Sidearm. The M4A1, Type 89, and M&P 15 may be carried as long guns. Non-lethal weapons such as the bean-bag shotgun, tazer, OC-spray, and baton can be carrised at the discretion of the trooper.

Use of Force

Force (both lethal and non-lethal) may only be used in proportion to the threat posed towards our personnel, and others surrounding. If the situation permits, less-lethal, and non-lethal options must be attempted unless. Troopers must respect the following priority of life when in high-risk situations.

  1. Officer/Operative/Trooper
  2. Hostage/Victim/Civilian
  3. Suspect(s).

Failure to comply with these regulations may lead to infractions, and possible removal from the department / sub-divisions if severe.

1.4 Quota

As a member of the Ghost Unit, you are required to meet a minimum shift time of 2 hours per week by at latest Saturday 15 UTC. Infractions waves may be delayed until Sunday at the discretion of HICOM. Accumulating more than 4 hours within a single wave renders you elligible for promotions if rank-section-related conditions are met. If you are unable to meet your quota or rank-section responsibilities, file for a Leave of Absence before Friday 23:59 UTC.

1.5 Traffic Stops & Arrests

When conducting traffic stops and arrests, troopers must follow all departmental guidelines and procedures. This includes proper documentation, in the form of logs with the Ghost Unit Utilities bot, ensuring safety of all parties involved, and maintaining professionalism throughout the process. Personnel must always identify themselves clearly with atleast their name and rank. Personnel must inform suspects of their rights when applicable, as well as the reason for the stop / arrest when earliest safe and possible to do so.

Troopers must always ensure they have adequate backup when dealing with potentially dangerous suspects. This includes requesting additional units from within the Ghost Unit and sub-divisions as well as other departments.

Personnel must have probable cause before initiating any traffic stop or arrest.

Failure to comply with these regulations may lead to infractions, and possible removal from the department / sub-divisions if severe.

1.6 Pursuits

Personnel are required to engange in soley in pursuits when deemed necessary to decrease the danger a suspect may pose. Pursuits must be terminated immediately if deemed unsafe to continue. The position and statuts of the pursuing units must be communicated clearly when safe to do so. Troopers must always prioritize the safety of themselves, other road users, and the suspect. Personnel may not overtake units ahead of them unless explicitly authorized to do so by the primary unit. Crashes must be roleplayed accordingly, and excessive force must be avoided. All FSRP rules must be followed at any time during pursuits.

1.7 Shift Management

All shifts must me logged with melonly when using any GU assets. The according shift type must be selected when starting a shift. Shifts must me paused or set "on break" when not actively patrolling or engaging in department-related activities. Shifts may be managed either via the melonly website or via a command channel. The following commands may be used with the bot:

  • /shift manage - Starts / stops / pauses shift
  • /shift leaderboard - Shows current shift leaderboard
  • /shift active - Lists all active shifts

1.8 Leave of Absence

Personnel may request a leave of absence via the melonly website or /loa requestcommand when unable to fulfill quota or duties. Approval is subject to the the following:

  • The request may not be longer than 4 weeks
  • The request may not be submitted within 1 week of a previous.
  • Requests submitted after Friday 23:59 UTC will not give immunity to shift infractions if quota is not met.
  • Probationary ranks may not file for leave of absences.
  • Request must contain at minimum a vague concievalbe reason.

During the duration of the Leave of Absence, personnel are exempt from quota and duty requirements. However, personnel are not permitted to participate in any department-related activities including but not limited to shifts, events, and trainings, with the exception of game nights. Failure to comply with these regulations may lead to infractions, and possible removal from the department / sub-divisions if repeated.

1.9 Promotions Requirement

Promotion eligibility requirements vary by rank. The following are general duties and requirements. Only applicable conditions are considered.

General Requirements

  • Meet promotion quota
  • Be off cooldown (between 1 and 10 weeks)
  • Engange semi-frequently with the community
  • Have no infractions in the week leading up to a promotion wave

HICOM Requirements

  • Be active in-game
  • Issue infractions when warranted
  • Handle resignation requests
  • Lead or manage a sub-division
  • Engage with the community
  • Handle tickets
  • Contribute to the server
  • Host or assist in mass deployments and events

SHR and HR Requirements

  • Be active in-game
  • Engage with the community
  • Handle tickets
  • Receive positive feedback in trooper reviews
  • Avoid being infracted
  • Issue / request infractions when warranted
  • Assist in events / deployments when requested
  • Assist in field training programmes

SGT Programme Requirements

  • Be active in-game
  • Handle tickets
  • Avoid being infracted
  • Host trainings and ride alongs
  • Supervise on dutys units

LR Requirements

  • Be active in-game
  • Avoid being infracted
  • Display professionalism

1.10 Infractions

Infractions are issued to maintain professionalism, fairness, and compliance with department and server standards. The following actions may result in an infraction. This list is non-exhaustive.

  • Failure to use proper spelling, punctuation, and grammar (SPaG) in public communications or in-game
  • Failure to meet community or department standards
  • Failure to end shift when not actively fufilling departmental tasks
  • Failure to follow lawful instructions from a High Rank
  • Abuse or misuse of authority or power
  • Failure to follow department policies or server rules
  • Unprofessional conduct, on-duty or off-duty in uniform
  • Failure to meet quotas / requirements without an approved Leave of Absence
  • Failure to correctly log traffic stops or arrest
  • Use of unauthorised firearms, vehicles, liveries, or uniforms
  • Violation of any service's terms of service
  • Initiating a traffic stop without probable cause
  • Conducting traffic stops on Whitelisted departments for traffic violations
  • Causing or escalating drama within the department or with other departments
  • Disrespectful behavior toward other members or departments
  • Failure to identify when safe to do so
  • Leaking internal documents, information, or channels

Severity, intent, repetition, and prior history will be considered when issuing infractions. Severe or repeated violations may result in suspension or removal from the department.
